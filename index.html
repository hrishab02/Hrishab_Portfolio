About

Who I Am

Most security candidates study threats. I build things to understand them.

I hold an MS in Computer Security & Privacy from Drexel University. Over the past two years, I've gone well beyond coursework — designing a custom log protocol over TLS 1.3, implementing Bitcoin's ECDSA signature scheme from first principles, exploiting buffer overflows in controlled environments, and analyzing 191,000+ network flows using graph theory to surface lateral movement paths.

At VisioneerIT, I used Aikido Security to identify and remediate vulnerabilities across legacy codebases and gained hands-on exposure to Heimdal for endpoint and network protection. I've also published two technical articles and a white paper — live on a company's website, read by their clients.

Network security is where I am headed — from packet-level analysis and protocol design to detecting lateral movement and hardening infrastructure at scale.
